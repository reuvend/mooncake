CHUNGCHEONG SONGPYEON

Chungcheong Province is know for its sweet pumpkin songpyeon, made with newly harvested autumn pumpkins. The pumpkins are sliced, dried and ground into powder. The pumpkin powder is mixed with non-glutinous rice powder and hot water to form the dough. Fillings can include chestnuts or roasted sesame seeds. Pumpkin songpyeon are visually appealing as they are made to look like mini pumpkins.

PREPARE THE INGREDIENTS

Cut your kabocha in half and scrape out the seeds. Then, cut into 8 slices and coat the inside with honey. Cover with a cheesecloth and a lid, and steam for 10 minutes. Scoop out the flesh. The kabocha skin is edible, but we use just the insides for the golden yellow color

Sift the rice powder and salt first, then sift the kabocha flesh. Combine thoroughly. Add honey and hot water. Knead and add hot water little by little if needed. When you pinch off some dough and it stays together but does not get sticky, that’s the right texture you want. Cover the dough to prevent drying and set aside.

FILLING AND ASSEMBLY

Blend kabocha, brown sugar, heavy cream, condensed milk, and milk together to make the paste for the inside of the songpyeon. Take out 1 1/2oz of the dough and make a flat circle. Add the paste in the middle of it and close it. Then, roll into a ball and make a pumpkin shape using a small stick.

STEAM

Boil water in a steamer. Boil the water over high heat. When it starts to steam, place the rice cakes in the steamer insert without the pieces touching one another. over and steam for about 20 minutes. Prepare a big bowl of cold water. Using a long-handed spoon or tong, remove the rice cakes from the steamer. Drop them in the cold water for a quick rinse. Transfer the rice cakes to a colander to drain. Coat the surface of the songpyeons with sesame oil and place a pumpkin seed on top of each songpyeon. Serve warm or at room temperature
