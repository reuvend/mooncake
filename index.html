MID-AUTUMN FESTIVAL


Historically, harvest festivals are held around the time of the main harvest of a given region. Given the differences in climate and crops around the world, harvest festivals can be found at various times at different places. Harvest festivals typically feature feasting, both family and public, with foods that are drawn from crops. In many Eastern and Southeastern Asian countries, a lunar calendar is used to measure seasons and events. The harvest festival in many Asian countries is referred to as the ‘Mid-Autumn Festival’ for where it falls on the lunar calendar.
